Branding Elements

What does the company do, or offer?

The company I'll be creating will be a themed cafe revolved around birds. The company will have a physical location where customers can visit to play and observe birds in the vicinity. During their visit, customers can order cafe items for themselves and/or purchase snacks (i.e. seeds, fruits, etc.) for the birds. The cafe also acts as a bird rescue, so customers who are interested may choose to adopt any available birds.

Our website will have the following pages (not finalized): home, about us, cafe menu, the birds, and booking a visit.

The 'Home' page will be the websites landing page, it will have a background image and navigation buttons that lead to the rest of the site. The "About Us" page will have information and story behind the company. On the same page, there can be a map showing where the physical cafe is located. There can even be information about the volunteers and staff that work on site as well as ways to get in contact with us.

The "Cafe Menu" page will contain a list of food and drink items available at our cafe. Users can browse the page to see prices, images and detailed description of the products. On this page, customers will also see the available bird feed and snacks.

The "The Birds" page will showcase the birds that are present at our cafes. In this section, each bird will have container/box on the page that features an image of them, their adoption status, their adoption fee, a detailed description, and a button that users can press to apply to adopt the bird. When they click this button, it will bring them to a new page where users can enter and submit their application. This application will be formated like a form (similar to the payment form example made for the style guide, but with different labels) with various input fields and labels.

Finally, the "Book a Visit" page will have a form that features drop-down menus. Users can choose a date and time that they would like to visit the cafe and use a button to confirm their booking.

Who is the company to potential customers?

The company's target audience will be students between the ages of 16-25, families, influencers and bloggers.

Students may find the cafe attractive because it will be a place that they can hang out and chat while in the presence of cute birds. It may not be the ideal place to hold study groups/sessions since some birds may be loud and distracting, but if it is a workable environment for them, they may also book a visit.

Families may also visit the location if they are looking to adopt a small pet. They can come in to get an idea of the responsibilies that are needed when taking care of a bird and learn more about bird care. They may also visit the cafe if they have recently viewed our website and wanted to meet a potential pet prior to adopting to ensure they the right fit.

Our cafe may also draw the interest of influencers and bloggers. These people can come to our cafe to help promote the adoption of these birds through their photos or their writing. They can come to our cafe to take photos of the birds to help the animals find a home quicker through exposure.

Example Persona: Stephanie (22)

Stephanie is a biology student who has goals of being a veterinarian. She is a busy student now that she is in her 3rd of university, she barely has time for herself, her friends or her family. This has caused her a lot of stress. She recently decided that she wanted to adopt a pet companion so she feels less lonely at home. She decided she wanted to try out having a pet bird but because she's never had one before, the idea was a bit daunting. Stephanie found our cafe and was able to book multiple visits with her friends and family and eventually fell in love with one of our cockatiels. She went to our website, submitted an application and eventually brought home her new companion.

Why might this company entice someone to purchase a product or service?

Customers can use our website to book a visit to our cafe. Once they have booked a visit, they can also use our website to browse through our menu for food and drinks that we offer as well as the birds that may be onsite on the day of their visit. Booking a visit will guarantee that we have the space for our visitors. Drop-in visits are allowed but booking will guarantee visitors a spot.

Since our target audience may be popular amongst students, we will offer a student discount or student special. Students are already struggling to find income to pay for school fees and housing so offering a discount may make a visit sound more worth their money.

Typography

The fonts for our website will be sans-serif fonts. This is because sans-serif fonts are seen as a more mordern font when compared to serif fonts. Sans-serif fonts also have better legibility for the web. Sans-serif fonts also fit our brand because they give a more casual and youthful vibe, introducing young customers to visit our website and feel welcomed.

Below are three fonts used for our website. "Josefin Sans" has been chosen as the font for our logo text because it has a goemetric feel to it making it distinct when compared to our other two fonts. It stands out in our navigation bar. "Merriweather Sans" is a font that looks great in smaller sizes. It would make great font to use for in the paragraph element. It comes in 8 styles so it will come in handy if we decide to use the font for headings as well. The next chosen font is "Be Vietnam Pro" font. This font has great readability and it seems to work well with "Merriweather Sans" especially when used as a heading.

Color Palettes

The colours that we want to go with our brand will be more natural and earth-y colours. These colours will provide a rustic yet cozy feeling which reflects our cafe's setting. Below are some of the colours that we explored:

  • Green: represents nature and restoration; provides a sense of calmness. Focus more on earthy-y greens rather than bright greens because it provides a deeper connection with nature.
  • Pink: provides a sense of softness and sweetness. Mostly used as an accent colour and as a soft pink to enhance the feeling of calm.
  • Brown: represents nature, organic and warmth. The lighter beige colours can work well as a background colour while darker browns may work well as headings or paragraph text.
Many of these colours complement each other well as they would be categroize split-complementry on the colour wheel (if we consider pink as a red-violet and brown as red-orange).

Below are the finalized colours for our website. The background colour is a beige colour to create a warm and inviting tone. The darker text colour used for the headings and paragraph text allows for great contrast between it and the background. The accent colours are green and pink used for the buttons and links. These colours stand out from the background and the base text so that users can be cued about their interactivity. I decided to also use a lighter beige colour for the background of the forms so that they stand out from the background. Having the forms stand out is important because it highlights the contents that are within the form.
